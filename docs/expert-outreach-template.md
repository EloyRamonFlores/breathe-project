## Template B — Industrial Hygienist (CIH credential)

**Subject:** Asbestos risk tool — would value a CIH perspective

Hi [Name],

I'm the developer of ToxinFree, a free public tool that estimates asbestos risk in
buildings based on country of construction, decade built, and building type. It's
designed for laypeople who can't afford a professional inspection but want to know
whether they should be concerned.

The core model is a weighted average (country factor 45 %, era 35 %, building type
20 %) mapped to four risk tiers (Low / Moderate / High / Critical). I've drawn the
factor values from EPA, WHO, and IBAS regulatory data, but I'd like a CIH to review
the logic before I pitch the tool to health organizations.

Would you be willing to take a quick look and tell me whether the risk stratification
passes the sniff test? I'm specifically uncertain about:

- Whether the 1960–1980 era deserves a full 1.0 multiplier vs. the 1940–1960 era
- Whether factory (1.0) vs. school (0.90) ordering is defensible for the building factor

Happy to share the full methodology doc. No fee expected — a brief "this looks
reasonable" or a citation suggestion would be enormously helpful.

[Your name]
ToxinFree — toxinfree.org
